About Me

I wrote my first song when I was eight, during what I thought was the best friendship I'd ever had. That inspired me to write a song called Best Friends. I produced it with my piano teacher, but when we were almost done it was summer break — and when the break ended we'd both kind of forgotten about it.

After that I didn't write another song for a while. Then when I was eleven I went to karaoke with my friends and they sang You Belong With Me by Taylor Swift. I had no idea who Taylor Swift was. The song got stuck in my head, I looked it up, and from there I was sucked down the rabbit hole that is Taylor Swift. That inspired me to write.

Honestly? Everything. Anytime something happens to me or to people around me, I write a song about it. A lot of the time I don't finish that song.

I also get inspiration in the strangest places. Like one day I was out skiing all day and got these lyrics stuck in my head with a really catchy melody. I skied ahead of my brothers and Dad, took off my glove, pulled my phone out of my pocket, and recorded a voice memo. As the day continued I kept getting more pieces until I had an entire song in like five or six voice memos. I still haven't put them together though...

Write. Just Write. I think people are right when they say just write. If you're going to be a musician, make sure you can play an instrument. Write your songs and practice performing them. Practice. Practice. Practice. Practice. Practice.

If you want to be an author, just write your book. Write it until it's done. Just finish the first draft — then you can edit it. As I tell my friend who is insisting on rewriting her whole book: you can rewrite the whole book later. When you're done you need to edit it. Get your friends to read it. Delete and add entire chapters. Do whatever. Just don't do that until AFTER you finish draft 1.
